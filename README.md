# Light-Up Dress / Outfit with Fiber Optics

How can you make almost any dress light up? An easy method is to use **fiber optics**, **RGB LEDs**, and **lots of hot glue**.

This project shows how to add glowing fiber optic effects to clothing in a way that can work for many different styles. While one tutorial cannot cover every possible design, this guide includes ideas for both a **feminine** and a **masculine** look.

## Project Planning

Before starting, plan your design carefully. It is best to read the full tutorial before making decisions.

When planning your look, think about:

- Whether you want to **modify the clothing itself**
- Or create a **detachable light-up piece**

For example:

- The light-up part of my dress was a **Velcro skirt** worn on top
- The **suspenders** and **bow tie** had lights hot glued onto them

You should also decide:

- Where you want the **ends of the fiber optics** to be, since that is where the light will shine
- Where you want the **fiber optic cables to run**, since that is where your **LEDs and wiring** will go

## Materials

- 1.5mm fiber optic cable
- 5mm RGB common cathode LEDs
- Electrical tape
- Heat shrink tubing
- Hot glue
- Clothing or accessories to modify
- Velcro (optional, for detachable sections)

## Building the Light-Up Outfit

Once you know where your LEDs will go and how the fiber optics will be distributed, you can begin building.

### 1. Prepare the Fiber Optics

For this project, I used **1.5mm optical fiber** bought on Amazon. It came rolled up, so the strands kept their circular shape and had to be straightened for the skirt.

Fiber optics can be shaped in many ways, but keep these things in mind:

- **Sharp bends** can diffuse the light and make it appear dimmer
- **Heating the cables** to force them into shape can make them brittle

### 2. Attach the Fiber Optics to the LEDs

After shaping the cables, the next step is connecting them to the LEDs.

Using a **5mm RGB common cathode LED**, I was able to attach **8 strands** of fiber optic cable to each light.

To do this:

1. Gather the fiber optic strands into a **small, tight bundle**
2. Make sure the ends are lined up closely on one side
3. Use **electrical tape** to hold the bundle together
4. Attach the bundle to the LED using **heat shrink tubing**
5. Secure the connection with **hot glue**
   - Add glue inside the bundle
   - Add glue along the edges of the heat shrink tubing

This helps create a stronger connection and improves light transfer from the LED into the fibers.

## Design Tips

- Plan the glowing endpoints carefully, since these create the visible lighting effect
- Keep wiring and LEDs in places that are easy to hide
- Detachable sections can make the outfit easier to wear and maintain
- Test your light placement before permanently gluing everything down

## Conclusion

With some planning, fiber optics, LEDs, and hot glue, you can turn almost any outfit into a light-up costume piece. Whether you are making a glowing dress, skirt, suspenders, or bow tie, the same basic method can be adapted to fit your style.
